Once upon a time, a little girl named Alexandra was born in Lisbon — though she would forever insist she is half Brazilian, despite carrying a surname that is unmistakably German. Perhaps fate was already hinting at the curious mixture of worlds that lay ahead. For six years later, in the German city of Bielefeld — a place most famous for not being famous at all — a boy named Kristoffer entered the world.

For many years, their paths did not cross. After spending time in Portugal, Italy, Brazil and New York, Ale eventually built her life beneath the sunny skies of Barcelona, while Kris went all the way to Mexico only to later settle in the far rainier streets of London. One might say they are just as different as the cities they call home. And yet, as everyone knows, opposites have a most curious habit of attracting one another.

And so it was that, one summer evening in Ibiza, destiny quietly intervened. They met at a dinner with mutual friends — a dinner Ale was very eager to attend and Kris was not eager to attend at all. As it happens, friends, Covid, Neymar and Anitta all played their own peculiar roles in the chain of unlikely events that brought them to the same table.

On the night of the wedding celebration, just after midnight, it will be exactly six years since that evening in Ibiza — August 29th, 2020 — when their story truly began.

Six years of a long-distance relationship that was not always easy, yet somehow brought them closer with each passing day. Six years of growing together, learning from one another, and slowly becoming not only partners, but the very best of friends.

And the rest, dear reader, is the story that continues. ✨

On Getting About

The city rewards those who venture on foot — much may be discovered at a most agreeable pace. For greater distances, Cabify or Uber shall prove most reliable. The castle itself lies but thirty minutes from the city centre.

Diversions Most Heartily Recommended

  • ✦  A promenade of the most leisurely sort through the Gothic Quarter and El Born
  • ✦  An audience with the Sagrada Família and Park Güell (prior reservation is most strongly advised)
  • ✦  A morning of great pleasure at the Boqueria market
  • ✦  A long and thoroughly unhurried luncheon in Barceloneta
  • ✦  An idle and most delightful Sunday in the Gràcia neighbourhood

Chapter VIII

Frequently Asked Questions

Where is the wedding taking place?

Castell de Sant Marçal, 110154 Acceso provisional, 41, 08290 Cerdanyola del Vallès, Barcelona.

Are children allowed?

Our wedding will be an adults-only celebration. We would love for you to fully relax, celebrate, and enjoy the day without any responsibilities.

What is the best way to get to the venue?

We will be providing a private shuttle bus from Barcelona city centre. Collection points and exact timings will be confirmed closer to the date — please keep an eye on this website for updates. Alternatively, Cabify, Uber or taxi takes approximately 30 minutes at around €30.

Is parking available at the venue?

Yes, on-site parking is available. If you are planning to drive, please let us know in advance.

Can I take photos during the ceremony?

Please refrain from taking photos during the ceremony vows — we have entrusted a professional photographer and videographer to capture every moment, and we would love for you to be fully present with us. Outside of the vows, feel free to take all the photos you wish!

What if I cannot attend?

If you are unable to join us, please RSVP "no" as soon as possible. While we would love to celebrate with you, we completely understand that circumstances can change.

What about dietary restrictions?

Please let us know when you RSVP. We will ensure the catering team is informed and happy to accommodate you.

What about wedding gifts?

Your presence is truly the greatest gift. Should you wish to contribute, details for a honeymoon contribution are in the section above. Thank you so much.
